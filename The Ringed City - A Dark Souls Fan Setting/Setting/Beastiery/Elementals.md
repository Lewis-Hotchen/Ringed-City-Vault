Natural energy is found everywhere in the world of The Ringed City. Pockets of over-staturated condensed energy ocassionaly gives rise to elementals. Wild natural energy that will try to consume more and grow. Some believe the [The Cycle](obsidian://open?vault=Ringed%20City%20Vault&file=The%20Ringed%20City%20-%20A%20Dark%20Souls%20Fan%20Setting%2FSetting%2FGeneral%2FDates%20and%20Time#Cycle), is to blame. The huge amount of natural energy condensed in the centre of the world has caused The Ringed City to become a perfect catalyst of unstable Natural Energy. 

*Much like the wind, natural energy will pass through harmlessly. Until that wind becomes a storm.* - Unknown

## Fire Wisps
	Extra

#### Description
Wispy, small and harmless from a distance; Fire Wisps are elementals created from an excess in fire. A small ball of fire only the size of a human fist, with 3 disconnected fiery tentacles spout from the "back" of the main body, it propells itself forward through the air, not unlike a jellyfish. Often found dancing and skimming on the edge of the Sun. Fire Wisps are attracted to the light of fire and are often found near fire. Those that stray away from a source, or if it goes out will use their natural energy to set things on fire, and on some rare accounts, people.

## Frost Corpse
	Extra

#### Description
In the deepest pits of cold locations, frost will consume those that are dead, creating a frost corpse. A frost corpse is inanimate and often hides in ice and snow. Craving more death it will ensare those that pass over, consuming them with frost. Any avid mountaineer will know to avoid frost corpses, as without someone else to pull you out, it is certain death. A frost corpse can comprise of multiple corpses spread out over ~5 metres away from the original source of frost natural energy.

#### Description
Sharp, thin needle-like manifested blood. Operating as a swarm, they aim to lacerate and pierce to gain more blood for the swarm. Swarms tend to have up to 100 individual needles, making them extremely deadly and will rend flesh easily, like a school of bloodthirsty fish.
